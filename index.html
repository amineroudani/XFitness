Get started

The biggest question holding someone back from going to the gym is, what do I do? Going to the gym can be very overwhelming. The gym can be a scary place and you might not know what to do. How should I train? What exercises should I do? What should I eat? How often should I train? Am I sleeping enough? What do I do if I don’t have enough time to go to the gym? You can get lost asking yourself these questions but don’t worry, all the answers to those questions can be found on this exact site or you could just ask us and we are happy to help (don’t be shy). Let's break this down for a second. There are 3 main pillars to training (and this applies to every single physical activity).
  • Training
  • Nutrition
  • Recovery
All 3 pillars are essential to making making progress and achieving your goals.

Be consistent with these pillars, and we garantee that you will make incredible progress.

Let's look at these in more detail
Training
- Sustainability: choose a workout routine that you can actually stick to.
- Effort: Train close to failure while keeping good form and full range of motion.
- Progressive overload: You should increase weight and/or number of reps over time.
Nutrition
- Calories: make sure you are eating enough calories. A car can't drive without fuel.
- Protein: aim for 2g protein per kg body mass.
Recovery
- Sleep: non-negotiable 8 hours of sleep every night.
- Rest-days: your muscles "rebuild themselves" when you rest them after a workout. If you don't rest enough, it will be counter-productive. Make sure you listen to your body and allow for adequate rest between workouts.
